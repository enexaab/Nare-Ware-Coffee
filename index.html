Our Story

Founded in 2005, Nare Ware Coffee is a family-owned business with deep roots in the coffee-growing regions of Ethiopia. Our journey began in the highlands of Sidamo, where our founder's family has been cultivating coffee for generations.

We take pride in our Ethiopian heritage and the exceptional quality of our coffee beans. Our mission is to share the rich flavors of Ethiopian coffee with the world while supporting sustainable farming practices and improving the lives of local communities.

Today, Nare Ware Coffee is a leading exporter of premium Ethiopian coffee, supplying specialty coffee shops, roasters, and distributors worldwide. We maintain direct relationships with our network of small-scale farmers, ensuring fair compensation and sustainable growing practices.

Our Commitment to Sustainability

We believe in responsible farming practices that protect the environment and support local communities.

Environmental Responsibility

  • Shade-Grown Coffee

    Our coffee is grown under the natural canopy of native trees, preserving biodiversity and providing habitat for local wildlife.

  • Water Conservation

    We implement water-saving processing techniques and water recycling systems to minimize our environmental impact.

  • Organic Farming

    We use only organic fertilizers and natural pest control methods, avoiding harmful chemicals that damage the ecosystem.

  • Renewable Energy

    Our processing facilities are increasingly powered by solar energy, reducing our carbon footprint.

Community Support

  • Fair Compensation

    We pay our farmers above-market prices, ensuring they receive fair compensation for their hard work and exceptional coffee.

  • Education Programs

    We invest in education for farmers' children and provide training in sustainable farming practices and business skills.

  • Healthcare Access

    We support local healthcare initiatives, ensuring our farming communities have access to essential medical services.

  • Women's Empowerment

    We actively promote gender equality through training and leadership opportunities for women in coffee farming communities.
